## Reflection

Problem Set 4 was particularly valuable because it required combining several core heat transfer concepts into a single modeling framework. In the first problem, I developed a thermal resistance network for a photovoltaic panel that included conduction through multiple layers, contact resistance between materials, and both convection and radiation heat transfer at the exposed surface. Constructing the resistance network helped me better understand how complex thermal systems can be simplified into solvable models while still capturing the essential physics governing heat transfer.

The later problems focused on fin analysis and required calculating fin efficiency, fin effectiveness, and overall heat transfer rates for extended surfaces. These problems reinforced the importance of understanding when assumptions such as insulated tips or one-dimensional conduction are appropriate. More importantly, the assignment required interpreting results and ensuring that they were physically reasonable rather than simply applying equations mechanically. Working through these problems improved my ability to translate physical heat transfer situations into structured analytical models, a skill that will be important in future engineering design and thermal management problems.
